Research

In my past research experience, I've broadly been interested in machine learning, computer vision, and robotics. Most of my past research experience is within deep reinforcement learning where I have worked on model-free (e.g., DQN) and model-based approaches (e.g., World Models). I believe a robot should use computer vision to perceive the world, self-supervised learning models to reason, and meta-learning to leverage past experience when solving new tasks. I dream of a future with autonomous robots in the real world that learn broad and general notions of intelligence with the ability to solve diverse tasks from few examples in changing environments. This entails coping with unlabelled data, learning from few examples, applying past knowledge, and adapting in a dynamic environment that changes over time. I want to explore more about deep reinforcement learning, imitation learning, transfer learning, meta-learning, and self-supervised learning. Ultimately, my research goal is to develop robots that perceive, think, and act on a human level when solving diverse tasks from few examples across changing environments. More recently, my industry experience has sparked a research interest in software engineering, programming languages, and computer systems.
